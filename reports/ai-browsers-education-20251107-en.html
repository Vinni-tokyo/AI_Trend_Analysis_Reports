üìñ Key Terminology

AI Browser: Next-generation browsing tool with natively integrated artificial intelligence features that can directly execute search, summarization, translation, and Q&A within the browser
Browser-Native AI: Design approach where AI is integrated as a core browser feature rather than through extensions or plugins (e.g., Brave Leo, Opera Aria)
Zero-Log Policy: Privacy protection policy that stores no user conversation history, IP addresses, or identifying information (adopted by Brave Leo)
Multi-Tab Context: Feature that comprehensively understands content across multiple browser tabs and PDF files to provide cross-cutting Q&A and summaries
FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act): U.S. federal law protecting privacy of student educational records; tools adopted by educational institutions must comply with this regulation
Accessibility Features: Assistive technologies enabling users with visual, hearing, or learning disabilities to access information equally (text-to-speech, image recognition, real-time translation, etc.)
AI-First Browser: Next-generation browser with AI features incorporated as core design philosophy from the start rather than as an afterthought (Arc, Perplexity Comet, etc.)
