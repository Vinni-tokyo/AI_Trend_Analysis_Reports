üìö Key Terminology

Agentic AI

Autonomous AI systems capable of independently planning, making decisions, and executing tasks without human intervention. Key features include Goal-Oriented Autonomy, Dynamic Replanning, and Multi-Tool Integration.

Domain-Specific Language Models (DSLMs)

Large language models fine-tuned for specific industries (finance, healthcare, legal, etc.). Higher cost efficiency and domain accuracy compared to general-purpose LLMs.

Multimodal AI

AI systems capable of processing two or more data types simultaneously (text, image, audio, video). Examples include GPT-4o and Gemini 1.5 Pro.

AI Governance

Framework ensuring ethical and legal compliance of AI systems. Key components include Data Lineage Tracking, Bias Auditing, and Explainability Requirements.

Edge AI

Technology executing AI inference on the device itself (edge) rather than in the cloud. Key advantages include ultra-low latency, data privacy, and offline functionality.

Neoclouds

Emerging cloud providers specializing in GenAI workloads. Examples include CoreWeave, Lambda Labs, and RunPod, challenging traditional hyperscalers.

Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)

System design methodology including human verification steps for high-risk AI decision-making. Mandatory component for EU AI Act high-risk systems.

Data Lineage Tracking

Technology documenting the origin, processing history, and usage purpose of AI training data. Mandatory requirement for AI regulatory compliance.
