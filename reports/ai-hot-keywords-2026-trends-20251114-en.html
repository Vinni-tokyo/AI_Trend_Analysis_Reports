Key Terminology

Agentic AI - Autonomous AI systems that independently set goals, plan, and execute without human intervention. AI that acts on its own, beyond simple response AI.
Domain-Specific Language Models (DSLMs) - Specialized AI models trained on specific industry/domain data. Superior to general LLMs in accuracy, cost, and security.
Multimodal AI - AI that simultaneously processes and generates 2+ data formats like text, images, audio, and video. Examples: GPT-4 Turbo, Google Gemini.
Edge AI - Technology that executes AI inference on the device itself (smartphone, IoT, wearables) rather than in the cloud. Advantages: low-latency, privacy, offline operation.
Neoclouds - New cloud providers specialized in GenAI workloads. Eroding hyperscaler (AWS, Azure, GCP) dominance, $20B revenue forecast for 2026.
Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) - Process where humans verify and approve when AI makes high-risk decisions. Core element of AI governance.
