5. Copyright and Licensing: "Significant Human Input" Requirement

Current Status: In January 2025, the U.S. Copyright Office declared that purely AI-generated compositions cannot be copyrighted. To receive copyright protection, "Significant Human Input" is required, including complex prompts, editing, live performance integration, and layering of human-composed sections.

Copyright Protection Requirements

‚Ä¢ Significant Human Input: Simple prompt input alone insufficient for copyright
‚Ä¢ Editing Required: Substantially modify or edit AI output
‚Ä¢ Live Performance: Add human vocals or instrument performances
‚Ä¢ Composition Layering: Integrate human-composed sections

Outlook: Copyright regulations are the biggest uncertainty factor in the AI music industry. Key Terminology

Text-to-Music - Technology where AI generates complete music tracks when users input natural language text prompts (e.g., Suno, Udio)
Royalty-Free - Music that can be used unlimited times without additional royalties once licensed. Can be used on YouTube, TikTok without copyright claim concerns
MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) - Standard format for storing music information as digital data. Notes, tempo, and instruments can be freely edited in DAW
Stems - Individual tracks that make up a song (vocals, drums, bass, guitar, etc.). Each stem can be edited or mixed separately
DAW (Digital Audio Workstation) - Software for music recording, editing, and mixing (e.g., Logic Pro, FL Studio, Ableton Live)
Significant Human Input - 2025 U.S. Copyright Office standard. AI music must include substantial human creative choices and editing to receive copyright protection
Instrumental - Music performed only with instruments without vocals. Mainly used as background music (BGM)
