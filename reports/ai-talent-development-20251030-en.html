2. Severe AI Skills Gap: Training Supply Shortage

Current State: According to OECD research, only 0.3% to 5.5% of current educational courses provide AI content. Employee readiness is also critical, with only 6% feeling "very comfortable" using AI tools, and Gallup surveys showing approximately one-third expressing clear discomfort. 44% of companies report significant skills disruption from automation and AI, with 87% of executives predicting major skills gaps.

Key Terminology

AI Essentials (Google) - Google's self-paced AI training program that can be completed in less than 10 hours. No prior knowledge required, focuses on AI productivity skills, and awards a Google certificate upon completion.
Azure AI Fundamentals (AI-900) - Microsoft's foundational AI certification. Proves basic AI concepts related to Azure, suitable for both technical and non-technical backgrounds. No data science or software engineering experience required.
WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) - U.S. federal law providing funding for workforce development and skills training. The 2025 executive order encourages leveraging funds for AI skills development and workplace training opportunities.
AI Skills Gap - The difference between skills needed to effectively use AI tools and systems versus current workforce capabilities. OECD notes that current training supply may be insufficient to meet demand for general AI literacy skills.
White House AI Education Task Force - Established by April 2025 executive order. Plans and implements the Presidential AI Challenge for youth and establishes public-private partnerships to provide K-12 AI education resources.
AI Literacy - Basic understanding of AI tools, concepts, and impacts. Includes the ability to interact with AI systems, understand their capabilities and limitations, and use AI responsibly in daily work.
Adaptive Learning - Educational approach that customizes educational content and experiences based on individual learner needs, performance, and preferences. In AI talent development, adjusts training paths based on technical background, learning pace, and career goals.
