From Indeed Hiring Lab to LinkedIn Economic Graph - The Evolution of Next-Generation Talent Intelligence Platforms

Key Trends Top 5

1. GenAI Skills Assessment Pioneer: Indeed Hiring Lab

Current Status: Indeed Hiring Lab has developed an innovative methodology analyzing 2,900 skills across 53.5 million US job postings. Their GenAI exposure assessment system quantifies how much each skill can be automated by AI tools.

Large-Scale Skills Analysis Methodology

• 2,900 Skills Analyzed: Across 53.5M US job postings
• GenAI Transformation Index: 41% of skills exposed to high GenAI impact
• Job Impact: 26% of all jobs expected to radically transform

Real-Time Labor Market Insights

• January 2025 Data: 20% of job postings require high GenAI-exposed skills
• Growth Rate: Up 3 percentage points from 17% in 2023
• Industry Impact: Software, marketing, finance sectors highest

Related Links: Indeed Hiring Lab | Indeed

Outlook: Indeed Hiring Lab's research indicates the rapid AI transformation of labor markets, providing crucial metrics for companies planning employee reskilling initiatives.

2. Real-Time Economic Graph: LinkedIn Economic Graph

Current Status: LinkedIn Economic Graph has built a vast economic data network connecting over 1 billion members worldwide, 68 million companies, 41,000 skills, and 135,000 schools. This data is updated more than 5 million times per minute, capturing real-time talent flows.

Leveraging 1 Billion+ Member Data

• 1 Billion+ Members: Global member base
• 41,000 Skills: Standardized skills classification
• 5M Updates/Minute: Data update frequency

AI-Powered Skills Classification System

• Automatic Extraction: Auto-classifies skills from profiles and job postings
• 140% Increase: Skills addition rate since 2022
• 70% Change: Job skills will change by 2030

Related Links: LinkedIn Economic Graph | LinkedIn

Outlook: The Economic Graph tracks talent movement patterns between industries in real-time, becoming an essential tool for companies implementing skills-first hiring strategies.

3. AI Skills Disruption Matrix: Lightcast (formerly Emsi Burning Glass)

Current Status: Lightcast analyzes over 3 billion global job postings and 500 million career profiles to provide labor market data. Their AI Skills Disruption Matrix™ predicts how much each occupation will be transformed by AI.

1.3 Billion+ Job Postings Analysis

• 3 Billion: Cumulative job posting analysis count
• 500 Million: Career profiles
• 28% Premium: AI skill holders' salary increase ($18K annual increase)

Skills Gap Analysis

• 3x Faster Growth: AI/ML skills demand growing 3x faster than supply
• 51%: Majority of AI jobs outside IT roles
• 800% Growth: Generative AI roles in non-tech fields

Related Links: Lightcast | Lightcast AI Skills Outlook

Outlook: Lightcast's proprietary algorithms quantify skills gaps by region and industry, helping companies develop targeted AI skills training.

4. Strategic Workforce Planning: McKinsey Global Institute

Current Status: McKinsey has developed strategic workforce planning (SWP) methodologies in collaboration with Fortune 500 companies. SWP integrates business strategy, workforce demand forecasting, and skills gap analysis to create 3-5 year talent roadmaps.

Strategic Workforce Planning Framework

• 3x Revenue: Top 20% SWP-adopting firms achieve 3x average revenue per employee
• Operations-Finance-HR: Positioned at the intersection of three functions
• AI Integration: 25% of executives have GenAI roadmaps

Job Architecture Redesign

• Boundary Deconstruction: Shifting from traditional job boundaries to skill-based roles
• 32% Predict: 3%+ workforce reduction expected
• 13% Predict: Similar magnitude increase expected

Related Links: McKinsey Global Institute | Strategic Workforce Planning

Outlook: McKinsey Global Institute predicts that by 2030, 14% of the global workforce (approximately 400 million people) will need career transitions, providing scenario modeling tools to help companies balance automation and reskilling investments.

5. Global Talent Standards: World Economic Forum (WEF)

Current Status: WEF's Future of Jobs Report surveys over 1,000 companies representing 14 million workers across 55 countries. The 2025 report predicts that analytical thinking, AI/big data, and leadership will emerge as the most critical skills by 2030.

Future of Jobs Report 2025

• 55 Country Survey: 1,000+ companies, 14M workers
• 39% Change: 39% of existing skills will change by 2025-2030
• 78 Million: New job opportunities by 2030

Reskilling Revolution Initiative

• 1 Billion Goal: Provide education, skills, and job opportunities by 2030
• 350+ Participants: Companies and governments involved
• 85%: Employers prioritizing employee upskilling

Related Links: Future of Jobs Report 2025 | World Economic Forum

Outlook: Global leaders discuss talent policies at the annual Davos Forum. The 2025 main theme is 'Transition to a Skills-Based Economy,' promoting a hiring paradigm shift that values practical competencies over educational credentials.

Executive Summary

  • Data Scale is Key: LinkedIn Economic Graph (1B+ members) and Indeed Hiring Lab (53.5M job postings) dominate in real-time labor market data, while Lightcast (3B job posting history) excels in long-term trend analysis.
  • GenAI Redefines Talent Analytics: Indeed's GenAI exposure measurement, LinkedIn's AI skills classification, and Lightcast's AI Skills Disruption Matrix all present new methodologies for predicting how AI will transform talent markets.

Key Terminology

GenAI Exposure - A metric quantifying the likelihood that a specific skill or occupation will be automated or augmented by generative AI tools. Developed by Indeed Hiring Lab's methodology, expressed as a 0-100 score where higher values indicate greater AI impact.
Skills-First Hiring - A hiring approach that prioritizes practical skills over educational credentials or past job titles. According to LinkedIn Economic Graph research, companies using skills-first hiring expand their talent pool by 10x and increase diversity.
Strategic Workforce Planning (SWP) - A systematic approach that forecasts 3-5 year workforce demand in alignment with business strategy, analyzes skills gaps, and develops hiring, training, and organizational restructuring plans. A concept led by McKinsey.
Transferable Skills - Versatile skills that can be utilized when moving from one role to another. LinkedIn Economic Graph classifies data analysis, project management, and communication as highly transferable skills.
AI Skills Disruption Matrix™ - A tool developed by Lightcast that evaluates how much each occupation's skill requirements will change due to AI across three dimensions: 'replacement risk,' 'augmentation potential,' and 'new skill necessity.'
