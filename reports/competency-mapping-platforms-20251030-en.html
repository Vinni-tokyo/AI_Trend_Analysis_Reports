2. O*NET Skills Framework: US Department of Labor's Standardized Skills System

Current State: O*NET (Occupational Information Network) is a comprehensive occupational classification and skills framework managed by the US Department of Labor, categorizing over 1,000 occupations and 35 skills across 7 categories. It functions as a public standard widely adopted by educational institutions, government agencies, and enterprises.

Designed to enhance workforce mobility across EU member states and strengthen connections between education and the labor market.

Key Terminology

Skills Ontology: A structured framework mapping relationships between skills, occupations, and industries. Maps similar, alternative, and complementary skill relationships, forming the foundation for career paths and learning recommendations.
Competency Mapping: The process of systematically evaluating and visualizing organizational or individual skills and capabilities. Used for skills gap identification and career development planning.
Talent Marketplace: An internal market platform that analyzes employee skills in real time and matches them with new roles, projects, and learning opportunities.
Skills Intelligence: Technology leveraging AI and data analytics to predictively analyze labor market skills demand, supply, and trends. Supports hiring, development, and deployment decision-making.
Skills-Based Hiring: A recruiting approach emphasizing candidate actual skills and capabilities over educational background or work history. Major companies like IBM and Google have eliminated degree requirements, promoting skills-first hiring.
