2. Test-Time Training/Compute: The Inference-Time Revolution

Current State: Unlike conventional AI scaling laws (data size, model parameters), test-time training represents a new paradigm that temporarily updates model parameters during deployment to customize for specific tasks. MIT researchers achieved a 6x accuracy improvement using this method, while OpenAI's o1 model scored a perfect 12/12 at the 2025 ICPC World Finals, surpassing all human competitors.

Key Terminology

Neuromorphic Computing: Computer architecture mimicking the neuron and synapse structure of the human brain. Energy efficiency is 1,000x higher than conventional von Neumann architecture.
Test-Time Training: Technique that temporarily updates some model parameters during inference after deployment to customize for specific tasks. Can improve accuracy by 6x without additional training.
AI for Science (AI4S): Paradigm that uses AI to automate the entire scientific research process (hypothesis generation, experimental design, data analysis, paper writing). Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) are a representative technology.
Quantum-Neuromorphic Systems: Next-generation AI hardware combining quantum computing's superposition principle with neuromorphic spiking dynamics. Target commercialization in 2028-2030.
Spiking Neural Networks (SNN): Neural networks that transmit information through discrete spikes that fire over time. Mimics biological neuron operation with very high energy efficiency.
Catastrophic Forgetting: Phenomenon where neural networks forget previously learned information when learning new information. A core challenge in lifelong learning.
Brain-Computer Interface (BCI): Technology that directly reads brain signals to control computers or external devices. Divided into invasive (requires surgery) and non-invasive (helmet-type) approaches.
