1. eIDAS 2.0 Mandate: EU-wide Digital Identity Wallet Implementation by 2026

Current State: The EU's eIDAS 2.0 regulation entered into force on May 20, 2024, ushering in the era of the European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDI Wallet). Technical specifications were finalized in November 2024, and by November 21, 2026, all EU member states must provide citizens and businesses with at least one certified digital identity wallet option. Currently, four Large Scale Pilot projects involving 360 companies and public entities are underway. Austria has already deployed its EUDI Wallet, Valera, becoming one of the first countries to have a functional wallet before the 2026 deadline. From November 2027, companies and organizations requiring citizen identification or authentication must accept the EUDI wallet as a valid means of identification, with a goal of achieving 80% active adoption by 2030.

2. W3C Verifiable Credentials 2.0 Standardization: Enterprise Adoption Accelerates

Current State: In May 2025, W3C published Verifiable Credentials Data Model v2.0 as an official W3C Standard, rapidly accelerating enterprise adoption. VC 2.0 enables trusted and privacy-aware digital interactions across sectors including Health, Financial Services, Travel, Education, government identities, organization identities, and Smart Things identities (IoT). Microsoft Entra Verified ID uses both W3C VC and DID standards, focusing on interoperability through JWT-based VCs and OIDC for transport. In retail age verification, TruAge's core technology developed by NACS and Conexxus has been incorporated into Verifiable Credentials 2.0. In supply chain, GS1 indicates the technology has potential to make huge improvements in efficiency for data exchange between trading partners within and across borders.

4. Interoperability Standards Integration: eIDAS¬∑VC¬∑OIDC Convergence Accelerates

Current State: Interoperability integration of eIDAS 2.0, Verifiable Credentials, and OpenID Connect is accelerating. EUDI Wallets have adopted OpenID4VC (OID4VCI and OID4VP) as core protocols to standardize VC issuance and presentation. Microsoft Entra Verified ID implements interoperability in enterprise environments through JWT-based VCs and OIDC transport. The European Blockchain Services Infrastructure (EBSI) uses did:ebsi for legal entities and supports both JSON-LD and JWT formats as well as OIDC4VCI and DIDComm for transport. Businesses need to implement middleware or translation layers that enable interoperability between existing secure platforms (e.g., Hyperledger Indy/Aries) and EUDI-compliant standards (OpenID4VC, SD-JWT, mDoc).

Key Terminology

eIDAS (electronic IDentification, Authentication and trust Services) - EU regulation for electronic identification, authentication, and trust services. eIDAS 2.0 entered force in 2024, mandating all EU member states to provide digital identity wallets.
EUDI Wallet (EU Digital Identity Wallet) - EU digital identity wallet. Mobile application that securely stores driver's licenses, degrees, medical records, etc., and allows selective disclosure when needed.
Verifiable Credentials (VC) - W3C standardized verifiable credentials. Digital degrees, licenses, certificates, etc., that can be cryptographically verified, making forgery impossible.
OpenID Connect (OIDC) - Authentication protocol built on OAuth 2.0. Enables SSO (Single Sign-On) allowing access to multiple services with one account like Google or Microsoft login.
DID (Decentralized Identifier) - Decentralized identifier. Unique ID that individuals can create and manage directly without central authority, stored on distributed ledgers like blockchain.
SSI (Self-Sovereign Identity) - Self-sovereign identity. Identity model where individuals fully control their digital identity information and can selectively disclose it to third parties without central authority.
OID4VCI (OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance) - Protocol integrating OIDC and VC, securely issuing verifiable credentials using OIDC.
SD-JWT (Selective Disclosure JWT) - JWT (JSON Web Token) enabling selective disclosure. Protects privacy by allowing selective disclosure of necessary parts without revealing all information.
