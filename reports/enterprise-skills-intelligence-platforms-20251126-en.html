Platform Deep Dive Analysis

1. Workday Skills Cloud: Universal Ontology of 200K+ Skills

Current State: Workday Skills Cloud is a machine learning-based skill ontology launched in 2018. Starting with 25 million skill instances, it now processes over 5 billion skill data points. The key insight is that "200,000+ skills" are registered in the database, which are consolidated and normalized into 55,000 verified skills through machine learning.

Technical Architecture

• Graph Technology: Maps relationships between skills
• Machine Learning: Skill inference and auto-classification
• LLM-Based Service: Infers skills from job titles (2024)
• OOV Handling: Addresses new/unknown job titles

Scale and Data

• Skills DB: 200K+ skills (55K normalized)
• Worker Data: 45+ million workers
• Customers: 2,100+ companies
• Fortune 500: 30%+ adoption

LLM-Based Skill Inference (2024-2025 Innovation)

Workday developed a "Skill Inference" service powered by Large Language Models (LLMs). It automatically infers relevant skills from job titles and connects them to the Workday Skill Ontology. This solves the "Out of Vocabulary (OOV)" problem—handling new and unknown job titles that traditional approaches couldn't address. They implemented evaluation benchmarks using synthetic data verified by human annotators, enabling continuous model improvement.

Related Links: Workday Skills Cloud | LLM Skill Inference Blog | Skills Cloud Foundation

Outlook: Integration with Workday Illuminate (next-gen AI) will further advance skill understanding and talent deployment automation. Seamless integration within the HCM ecosystem remains its greatest strength.

2. Eightfold AI: Deep Learning on 1.6 Billion Career Profiles

Current State: Eightfold is a deep learning-based talent intelligence platform built on 1.6+ billion career profiles and 1.6+ million unique skills[1]. Its defining feature is AI matching that predicts "potential," not just past experience, covering the entire talent lifecycle from recruitment through development and internal mobility.

Deep Learning Architecture

• Neural Networks: Trained on 1B+ profiles
• Skills Ontology: 1.6M unique skills[1]
• Matching Variables: 50+ variables for scoring
• Bias Mitigation: Designed for fairness

Customer Results

• Bayer: 90% reduction in screening time
• Vodafone: 50% reduction in cost per hire
• Cloudflare: 30% improvement in sourcing productivity
• Activision: 47% of hires from talent rediscovery

How Potential-Based Matching Works

Eightfold's unique strength lies in predicting "Skill Adjacency." Even for skills candidates don't currently possess, the system calculates acquisition probability based on career trajectory. For example, it predicts a Python engineer's potential to transition to Data Scientist by analyzing similar career paths. This enables discovery of "hidden talent" that traditional keyword matching would miss.

Related Links: Eightfold AI | Talent Intelligence Platform | Platform Details

Outlook: As a unicorn valued at $2B+, Eightfold has also achieved government certification (FedRAMP). Further automation is expected with the introduction of agentic AI capabilities.

3. Gloat Skills Foundation: Real-Time Adaptation via Workforce Graph

Current State: Gloat unveiled Skills Foundation in April 2023, providing AI-powered skills infrastructure. Their proprietary "Workforce Graph" learns from billions of data points, integrating open web sources, government databases, partner data, and anonymized user data. The key differentiator is a skill ontology that dynamically evolves with market changes.

Workforce Graph Technology

• Graph-Based AI: Deep learning on work-skill-role relationships
• Data Sources: Open web, government DBs, partners
• Real-Time Updates: Auto-evolves with market trends
• HR System Integration: Harmonizes with existing data

Customer Results

• Seagate: 50% internal mobility success, $20M saved
• Standard Chartered: 60% of new roles filled internally
• Engagement: 25% increase in employee engagement
• Clients: Schneider Electric, PepsiCo, Nestlé, HSBC

Talent Marketplace Innovation

Gloat's Talent Marketplace matches employees with internal opportunities (projects, mentorships, career shifts, temporary assignments) based on their skills, aspirations, and goals. Rather than static job descriptions, dynamic skill-based matching creates an experience where "work finds people." As a Work Orchestration Platform, it orchestrates skills, work, and technology holistically.

Related Links: Gloat | Skills Foundation | Talent Marketplace

Outlook: Named to the 2024 Work Tech Vendor Top 25. Growing as a key player supporting internal mobility and the transition to skills-based organizations.

Selection Guide

When Workday Skills Cloud is Optimal

• Already using Workday HCM
• Consistency within single platform is priority
• Gradual skills strategy is sufficient
• Need to minimize additional costs

When Eightfold AI is Optimal

• Seeking cutting-edge AI technology
• Need unified view from hire to retire
• Want to enable potential-based hiring
• Large-scale talent discovery is a challenge

When Gloat Skills Foundation is Optimal

• Internal mobility and employee engagement are priorities
• Emphasis on gig work and project-based opportunities
• Need market-adaptive skill ontology
• Goal is to build a talent marketplace

Application Ideas

3. Recruitment Optimization - Use potential-based matching to discover candidates that traditional keyword searches would miss

Key Terminology

Skills Ontology - A knowledge system defining skill components and relationships between skills. Maps more complex relationships than simple taxonomy
Skill Inference - Technology where AI automatically deduces and extracts relevant skills from job titles or career history
Talent Marketplace - Internal platform matching employees with opportunities (projects, positions) based on skills
Potential-Based Matching - AI technique that matches based not just on current skills, but predicted acquisition likelihood and growth trajectory
Workforce Graph - Gloat's proprietary AI architecture that models relationships between work, skills, and roles using deep learning
