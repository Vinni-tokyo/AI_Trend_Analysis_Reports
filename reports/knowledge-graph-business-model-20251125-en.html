Key Terminology

Knowledge Graph - A data structure that represents information as "nodes" (entities) and "edges" (relationships), enabling semantic connections to be visualized and utilized. Used at scale by Google and Facebook.
Property Graph - A graph model where nodes and edges have properties (attributes). Used by Neo4j and Amazon Neptune. Known for flexible data modeling.
RDF (Resource Description Framework) - W3C standard semantic web technology. Expresses data in "subject-predicate-object" triple format. Used by Stardog and others.
Graph RAG - Technology combining knowledge graphs with generative AI. Enables accurate, context-based response generation. Implemented in Amazon Neptune + Bedrock and similar platforms.
KaaS (Knowledge-as-a-Service) - A business model offering knowledge graph construction and operation as a fully managed service. Provided by Diffbot and others.
Ontology - A knowledge model that systematically defines concepts and their relationships in a specific domain. Functions as a "blueprint" for knowledge graphs, ensuring semantic consistency of data.
