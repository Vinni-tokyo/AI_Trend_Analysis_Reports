Knowledge Representation in the AI Era:
Ontology and Knowledge Graph Technology Trends

Graph-Based Knowledge Representation Research for Next-Generation AI
Report Date: November 25, 2025 | Standard Report

Top 5 Key Trends

1. GraphRAG: Fusion of LLM and Knowledge Graphs

Current Status: GraphRAG is rapidly gaining adoption as an innovative approach that overcomes the limitations of traditional vector-based RAG and provides LLMs (Large Language Models) with advanced reasoning capabilities. According to Microsoft Research, GraphRAG improves query speed by up to 5x compared to traditional RAG and reduces infrastructure costs by 40%.

Technical Features

• Context Understanding: Clarifies entity relationships for context-appropriate accurate responses
• Evidence Tracking: Enables clear tracing of data sources behind answers
• Scalability: Efficient operation even with graphs containing billions of nodes

Business Value

• ROI 300-320%: Proven in finance, healthcare, and manufacturing
• Hallucination Reduction: Significantly reduces LLM hallucinations through structured knowledge
• Real-time Updates: Enables immediate reflection of dynamic information

Related Links: Microsoft GraphRAG | Neo4j LLM Knowledge Graph Builder | MongoDB Atlas GraphRAG

Outlook: From 2025 onwards, GraphRAG is expected to become the standard architecture for enterprise AI systems, particularly becoming essential technology for private data analysis.

2. Neuro-Symbolic AI: Enhancing Reliability Through Ontology

Current Status: Neuro-Symbolic AI integrates the flexibility of deep learning with the rigor of symbolic reasoning to improve the reliability and explainability of AI systems. 2025 research combines OWL ontologies with reasoning engines like HermiT to significantly improve LLM output consistency.

Role of Ontology

• Reference Modeling: Formal representation of domain knowledge
• Common-sense Reasoning: Defining tacit knowledge as explicit rules
• Knowledge Refinement: Managing and simplifying complex conceptual relationships

Research Outcomes

• Prediction Quality Improvement: Demonstrated accuracy improvements in ontology-based neural networks
• Explainability: Making AI decision processes understandable to humans
• Data Efficiency: Achieving high accuracy learning with minimal data

Related Links: Neuro-Symbolic Integration Research | W3C OWL Standard

Outlook: As demand for explainability and reliability increases, Neuro-Symbolic AI is expected to accelerate adoption in regulated industries (healthcare, finance).

4. Evolution of Graph Visualization Tools

Current Status: Graph visualization technology is evolving from expert-oriented tools to intuitive tools for business users. Various tools are available for different purposes, including Neo4j Bloom, Cytoscape.js, and D3.js.

Tool Categories

• Business: Neo4j Bloom (explore without Cypher)
• Research/Analysis: Cytoscape (biology, network analysis)
• Developer: D3.js, Cytoscape.js (advanced customization)

2025 New Technologies

• Cosmograph: WebGL acceleration for fast rendering of hundreds of thousands of nodes
• NVL: Neo4j's official visualization library
• react-force-graph: React-integrated force simulation

Related Links: Neo4j Bloom | Cytoscape.js | D3.js | Cosmograph

Outlook: With the spread of WebGL/GPU acceleration technology, real-time visualization of large-scale graphs in browsers will become standard, expanding their use as decision support tools.

5. Knowledge Graph Embedding Through GNN (Graph Neural Networks)

Current Status: GNNs are gaining attention as a technology that effectively integrates knowledge graph structural information into deep learning models. Various architectures such as GCN, GAT, and RGCN have been developed and are being practically applied in recommendation systems and drug discovery research.

Key Architectures

• GCN: Aggregates neighbor information through convolution operations
• GAT: Dynamically weights important relationships through attention mechanisms
• RGCN: Efficient processing specialized for multi-relational graphs

Application Areas

• Recommendation Systems: High-precision prediction of user-product relationships
• Drug Discovery: Drug-target protein interaction prediction
• Fraud Detection: Anomaly detection in financial transaction patterns

Related Links: Deep Graph Library (DGL) | PyTorch Geometric

Outlook: 2025 research (KSG-GNN) proposes an approach that transforms knowledge graphs into homogeneous graphs, enabling direct application of existing GNNs and significantly lowering barriers to practical implementation.

Key Terminology

Knowledge Graph - A database that represents entities (people, places, concepts, etc.) and their relationships in a graph structure. Term introduced by Google in 2012.
Ontology - A formal definition of concepts, properties, relationships, and constraints in a specific domain. OWL (Web Ontology Language) is the standard language.
GraphRAG - An architecture that integrates knowledge graphs into LLM's Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Enables high-precision answer generation through entity relationship understanding.
Neuro-Symbolic AI - An AI approach that integrates neural network learning capabilities with the rigor of symbolic reasoning. Also called Third Wave AI.
GNN (Graph Neural Network) - A neural network capable of directly processing graph-structured data. Learns node features and adjacency relationships.
RDF/SPARQL - RDF is a W3C standard for expressing data as subject-predicate-object triples. SPARQL is a language for querying RDF graphs.
