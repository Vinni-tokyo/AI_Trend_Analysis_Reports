5. Adoption Barriers and Maturity Assessment Transition

Gradient-based training of deep spiking neural networks is now an off-the-shelf technique, supported by open-source tools and theoretical results. However, barriers persist including programming paradigm shifts, lack of standardization, and high costs.

Major Adoption Barriers

‚Ä¢ Programming Paradigm: Steep learning curve for event-driven, spike-based encoding, asynchronous timing

‚Ä¢ High Cost: Slow commercialization rate, expensive chips

Key Terminology

INRC (Intel Neuromorphic Research Community): Intel's neuromorphic research community. Essential membership program for Loihi chip access. Academic institutions get 1-year free loans, commercial/government organizations can purchase.
Spiking Neural Networks (SNN): Neural networks that transmit information through discrete spikes firing over time. Mimics biological neuron operation with event-driven approach for very high energy efficiency.
E-skin (Electronic Skin): Integrates tactile sensors into robotic hands enabling human-level delicate object manipulation. Combined with neuromorphic tactile processing for real-time sensory feedback.
Event-driven Computing: Computational approach performing operations only when events occur. Unlike conventional clock-driven methods, consumes power only when needed for high energy efficiency.
ADAS (Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems): Currently the largest commercial market for neuromorphic chips, utilized for real-time object detection and collision avoidance.
In-memory Computing: Computational approach performing operations directly inside memory. Processes without data movement, dramatically improving energy efficiency and speed. Core technology for neuromorphic architecture.
BCI (Brain-Computer Interface): Technology directly reading brain signals to control computers or prosthetics. Neuromorphic computing's low-power characteristics enable portable BCI device realization.
