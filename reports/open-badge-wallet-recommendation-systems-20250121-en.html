Top 5 Key Trends

1. Credly Wallet: AI-Powered Smart Recommendations

Current State: Credly, a Pearson subsidiary, is the world's largest digital badge platform, offering an AI-powered smart recommendation system. It provides personalized recommendations for badges and learning paths based on users' career goals.

Recommendation System Features

• Smart Badge Recommendations: AI suggestions based on current skills and career goals
• Skills Wallet: Dynamic display of verified skills
• Badge Saving: Track badges you want to earn in the future

Business Impact

• Interview Invites: 18% increase for badge holders
• Referral Rate: 40% increase with wallet integration
• Skills Gap Analysis: Enterprise feature

Related Links: Credly | Credly App | Credly Learn

Outlook: Credly's global network continues to strengthen AI-based career path recommendations. Integration with enterprise talent development programs is accelerating.

2. Territorium LifeJourney: Labor Market Data-Integrated Recommendations

Current State: LifeJourney, developed by Territorium, is an advanced digital wallet that leverages the latest labor market data for career path recommendations. It integrates with Comprehensive Learner Records (CLR) to achieve optimal matching between job seekers and job openings.

Recommendation Engine Features

• AI-Generated Pathways: Identifies 5 critical skills for specific positions
• Opportunity Fit Score: Quantifies skill match with job listings
• Learning Experience Recommendations: Suggests paths to achieve career goals

Interoperability

• Digital Credential Management: Unified management of badges, microcredentials, CLRs
• Multi-Protocol Support: Import/export capabilities
• Up to 5 Pathways: Track multiple career paths

Related Links: LifeJourney | Territorium

Outlook: LifeJourney is expanding partnerships with higher education institutions. Multiple universities including Kean University have adopted it as a skills-focused solution.

3. LearnCard Wallet: Open Standard Recommendation Foundation

Current State: LearnCard, developed by Learning Economy Foundation, is an open-source wallet based on W3C Verifiable Credentials and DIDs. Through Skills Bridge, it enables connection to decentralized Learning and Employment Record (LER) networks.

Skills Bridge Functions

• Store: Secure storage of skills text
• Discover: Find and explore related skills
• Map: Link skills to career paths

Ecosystem Integration

• Credential Engine Partnership: Leverages Linked Open Data
• ScoutPass: Official World Scouting wallet (250K+ badges issued)
• SuperSkills!: Children's game with LEGO Foundation

Related Links: LearnCard | Learning Economy Foundation | Documentation

Outlook: LearnCard, as a nonprofit organization, aims to achieve skills equity. Its open standards-based approach has the potential to become the foundation of the global LER ecosystem.

4. Workday WayTo: Enterprise Skills Cloud Integration

Current State: WayTo by Workday is an enterprise solution that achieves blockchain-based credential management and integration with Skills Cloud, used by over 30% of Fortune 500 companies.

Skills Cloud Recommendation Features

• AI Skills Extraction: Auto-extract skills from resumes and experiences
• Personalized Suggestions: Discover skill connections and recommend
• Talent Pool Expansion: AI job recommendations for candidate discovery

Talent Marketplace

• Skills Matching: Place talent in projects, gigs, opportunities
• Career Hub: Skills-based role suggestions
• Blockchain Verification: Instant credential verification

Related Links: Skills Cloud | Workday Credentials

Outlook: Powered by Workday Illuminate™ AI, skills recommendation accuracy is improving. Real-world effectiveness is proven, such as reducing nurse onboarding at hospitals from weeks to days.

5. TalentPass: AI Conversational Skills Discovery

Current State: TalentPass, developed by Gobekli, features Pythia, a conversational AI assistant that helps users discover and articulate their skills. The Talent Tree provides a visual skills map representing the full scope of one's potential.

Pythia AI Assistant

• Conversational Data Collection: Gather skills info through conversation
• Skills Quantification: Help quantify possessed skills
• Goal Discovery: Support career goal clarification

Passport Pages

• School Integration: Data exchange with educational institutions
• Employer Integration: Credential sharing with companies
• Cross-Wallet Connection: Interoperability of verifiable talent data

Related Links: TalentPass | Gobekli

Outlook: A successful pilot project with University of Phoenix is accelerating efforts toward realizing an AI-driven LER ecosystem.

Key Terminology

CLR (Comprehensive Learner Record) - A format that comprehensively records a learner's entire history, including grades, credentials, skills, and activities.
LER (Learning and Employment Record) - A digital format integrating learning and employment records, accumulated throughout one's career.
W3C VC (Verifiable Credentials) - An international standard for digital credentials established by W3C. Uses cryptographic technology to prevent tampering.
DID (Decentralized Identifier) - Decentralized identifier. Technology enabling individuals to manage their own IDs without a central authority.
SSI (Self-Sovereign Identity) - Self-sovereign identity. The concept where individuals have complete control over their own data.
Open Badges 3.0 - The latest version of digital badge specification established by 1EdTech. Compatible with W3C VC.
