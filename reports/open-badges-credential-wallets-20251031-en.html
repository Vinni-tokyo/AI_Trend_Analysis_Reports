Key Terminology

Open Badges 3.0 - Latest version of the international standard for digital badges. Enables more detailed and secure credentials through encryption capabilities, skills framework alignment, and assessment metadata
Verifiable Credentials - W3C-standardized format enabling cryptographic verification of digital credentials' authenticity. Issuer's signature prevents tampering
EUDI Wallet (EU Digital Identity Wallet) - Government-provided digital ID wallet for EU citizens to securely store and share educational credentials, driver's licenses, medical records, and more
Micro-Credentials - Short-term certifications proving mastery of specific skills or knowledge. More granular than traditional degrees, directly demonstrating skills employers seek
Selective Disclosure - Technology enabling individuals to share only necessary portions of their credentials. For example, presenting "over 18" information rather than full date of birth for age verification
DID (Decentralized Identifier) - New identifier system enabling individuals to manage their identity without relying on centralized databases. Utilizes blockchain or distributed ledger technology
Learning and Employment Records (LER) - Integrated digital records combining learning history and employment history. Enables unified management of lifelong learning and career trajectories for use in job transitions and career changes
