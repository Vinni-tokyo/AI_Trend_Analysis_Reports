Key Terminology

Open Badges 3.0 - Digital badge standard specification released by IMS Global in November 2022. Compatible with W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model v2.0, enabling verification via cryptographic proofs and portability.
Verifiable Credentials (VCs) - W3C standard tamper-evident digital credentials. Cryptographically protected, functioning through a three-party trust model of issuer, holder, and verifier.
Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) - Globally unique identifiers created and controlled by users without central authority dependence. Stored on blockchain, ensuring immutability and security.
Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) - Self-sovereign identity model where individuals create, manage, and own digital identities. No dependence on centralized databases.
Micro-credentials - Small-scale credentials proving specific skills or abilities. Obtainable in shorter timeframes than traditional degrees, valued by 72% of employers during hiring.
Learning Pathways - Feature clearly mapping connections between multiple badges, combining smaller "micro" achievements into larger "macro" credentials.
Cold Start Problem - Challenge where educational institutions hesitate when launching digital badging initiatives due to lack of understanding or technical barriers. Being resolved through AI automation.
EBSI (European Blockchain Services Infrastructure) - EU blockchain initiative aiming to make digital IDs and credentials universally accepted across European borders.
