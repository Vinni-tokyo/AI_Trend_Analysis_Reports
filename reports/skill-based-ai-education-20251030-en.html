Key Terminology

Micro-Credentials: Small-scale qualifications focused on specific skills or knowledge domains, obtainable in short timeframes. More flexible than traditional degrees and directly aligned with employer needs.
Digital Badges: Digital credentials that can be issued and shared online. Include metadata (issuer, date earned, skill content) and are blockchain-verifiable.
Skills-First Hiring: Hiring approach prioritizing demonstrated skills and abilities over degrees or credentials. Adopted by IBM, Google, Microsoft and others.
LXP (Learning Experience Platform): System integrating AI-powered personalized learning, skills development, and career path recommendations.
Competency Mapping: Technique for visualizing employee skills within organizations and analyzing gaps against job requirements. Increasingly automated and real-time through AI.
Stackable Credentials: Structure allowing multiple micro-credentials to combine toward larger qualifications or degrees. Provides flexible learning pathways.
AI Tutor: Personalized tutoring system leveraging generative AI like GPT-4. Analyzes learner strengths/weaknesses to provide real-time feedback and customized learning paths.
