Key Terminology

Skills Ontology - A database that hierarchically classifies and systematizes all skills within an organization. Enables linking related skills and visualizing required competencies. Fuel50 maintains 5,000+ capabilities through an I/O psychologist team.
Micro-Credentials - Short-term, intensive certifications specialized in specific skills or knowledge domains. More flexible and quicker to obtain than traditional degrees, valued by 72% of employers in hiring decisions. Google and IBM are major providers.
LXP (Learning Experience Platform) - An evolved learning platform from traditional LMS. Features AI-based personalized recommendations, skills mapping, and multi-source learning content integration. Market projected to grow from $2.8B in 2025 to $28.9B by 2033.
Adaptive Learning - A learning system that analyzes learner performance data in real-time and dynamically adjusts difficulty, content, and learning paths. AI technology enables 40% reduction in learning time and improved engagement.
Competency Mapping - A process that clarifies skills, knowledge, and behavioral characteristics required for jobs and visualizes gaps with employees' current state. In 2025, evolving from descriptive tools to predictive and adaptive strategic foundations.
Skills-First Hiring - A hiring policy that prioritizes practical skills and capabilities over educational background or degrees. Major companies like IBM, Google, Microsoft eliminating degree requirements and practicing. Rapidly spreading across public and private sectors.
Talent Marketplace - A platform that AI-matches internal talent with projects/roles. Provided by Fuel50 and Gloat, simultaneously realizing employee career development and organizational optimal talent placement.
