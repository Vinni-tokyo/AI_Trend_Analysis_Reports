Key Terminology

Micro-SaaS - Small-scale SaaS businesses specialized in specific niche markets or functions. Operable by solo founders or small teams at $39-$199/month price points. In corporate training, includes compliance specialists, sales training experts, etc.
Product Engineer - Developer responsible for entire product outcomes beyond just coding, including business understanding, data analysis, A/B testing, and user behavior tracking. With AI tools, one person can achieve productivity equivalent to traditional small teams.
Microlearning - Learning method delivering knowledge through 3-10 minute short-form content. Achieves 80% completion rate and 70% knowledge retention improvement compared to traditional long-form courses (20% completion). Mobile-optimized and becoming mainstream in corporate training.
Best-of-Breed - Approach of combining the best specialized tools for each functional area rather than using a single comprehensive system. In corporate training, integrates multiple tools like LMS + microlearning + skills management + analytics.
Skills-Based Approach - Method of learning, evaluating, and managing based on individual skills rather than traditional course/curriculum focus. 78% of companies plan to use digital learning platforms for skills management in 2025.
GitHub Copilot - AI pair programming tool developed by Microsoft and OpenAI. Assists with code completion and generation, improving developer productivity by up to 55%. 15 million users, adopted by 90% of Fortune 100 (2025).
API-First Design - Approach of designing products centered on APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) to facilitate integration with other systems. Foundation technology enabling Micro-SaaS to connect with enterprise systems and build Best-of-Breed ecosystems.
